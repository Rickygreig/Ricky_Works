About Me

A little bit about myself; I have been an ICU nurse for the past 5 years or so. During that time I quickly sharpened many skills that have proven to be valuable in programming. My critical thinking skills allow me to pick apart and break hard problems down into more managable ones. While my leadership and communication skills allow me to work seemlessly as part of a team.

Charge Nurse

As a chanrge nurse I was in charge of keeping an updated patient list, as well as admiting and discharging patients on and off the unit. This role required detail contric communitcation between me, the other nurses on the unit, house supervisors, as well as doctors and residents. Given the nature of an ICU setting, the status of patients can change moment to moment, so close collaboration and immediate action was critical.

Lead Preceptor

Another role I had during my nursing career was lead preceptor for the ICU. I would precept all new hires for at least part of their orientation, if not all of it. I found training new grads the most rewarding. They were a blank slate so any thing I taught them had the potential to effect their practice indefinetly. I believe this role helped me realize what makes a good learner, as well as what it takes to be a good teacher/ mentor.

What Now?

I had come to a point in my nursing career where I decided I needed to start making some career moves. I searched around a bit and none of the options were very apealling. In my mind I had two realistic options, go into hospital administation or go back to school for a masters degree. Neither of those options seemed very appealing at the time, this is when I started to explore the world of programing.

Programing

I always had a curriosity for programing and developing, but the opportunity never fully presented itself. In highschool I took an intro to programing class, however I wasn't hooked. The class wasn't focused on problem solving and it didn't make programing an attractive career. Fastforward to now, and it is proving to be an amazing opportunity. In April this year I made the decision to hit the breaks on my nursing career and comit to coding full time. I am focusing on HTML, CSS, and Javascript to start, but I plan to begin developing with Python and other languages very soon.
