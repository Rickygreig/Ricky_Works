About Me

A little bit about myself; I have been an ICU nurse for the past 5 years or so. During that time I quickly sharpened many skills that have proven valuable in programing. I developed strong leadership, managerial, and critical thinking skills throughout my career.

Charge Nurse

As a chanrge nurse I was in charge of keeping an updated patient list, as well as admiting and discharging patients on and off the unit. This role required detail contric communitcation between me, the other nurses on the unit, house supervisors, as well as doctors. Given the nature of an ICU setting, the status of patients change moment to moment, so swift collaboration and action is critical.

Lead Preceptor

Another role I had during my nursing career was lead preceptor for the ICU. All new hires would be with me for at least part of their orientation, if not all of it. I found training new grads the most rewarding. They were a blank slate so any thing I taught them had the potential to effect their practice indefinetly. I believe this role helped me realize what makes a good learner, as well as what it takes to be a good teacher/ mentor.

What Now?

I had come to a point in my nursing career where I decided I needed to start making some career moves. I searched around a bit and none of the options were very apealling. In my mind I had two realistic options, go into hospital administation or go back to school for a masters degree. Neither of those options seemed very appealing at the time, especially when considering my ICU had been through 4 managers in my 5 years there.

Programing

I had always had a curriosity for programing and developing, but the opportunity never fully presented itself. In highschool I took an intro to programing class, however I wasn't hooked. The class wasn't that great and it didn't make programing an attractive career. Fastforward 10 years to now, and it is proving to be an amazing opportunity. In April this year I made the decision to hit the breaks on my nursing career and comit to coding full time. I am focusing on HTML, CSS, and Javascript to start, but I plan to begin developing with Python and other languages very soon
